how scoring works
Each event gets two independent scores (0–100), shown as marks on every card (exact numbers under "the numbers"):
🍸 booze — an LLM reads the full event listing and estimates the probability of free drinks, citing its evidence. The five-segment meter is that probability.
logistics — starts at 50, then time-of-day, neighborhood, and RSVP friction adjust it (e.g. +25 weeknight sweet spot, −30 waitlisted).

Event-type preference isn't a score — it's the tags filter in the sidebar. Pick the tags you're interested in and only matching events show up (events with no tags at all are never hidden by it).

The gold ring on each card is the match: the booze↔logistics blend at your slider's balance. A bright, glowing card is a confident pick (≥{{ (scoring_info.confident * 100) | int }}, capped at 3 per week); dimmed cards are maybes (≥{{ (scoring_info.maybe * 100) | int }}); anything below is hidden. Thresholds live in scoring/weights.py.
