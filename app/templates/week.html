how scoring works
Each event gets a 🍸 booze score (0–100), shown as a mark on every card (exact number under "the numbers"): an LLM reads the full event listing and estimates the probability of free drinks, citing its evidence. The five-segment meter is that probability.

Event-type preference isn't a score — it's the tags filter above. Pick the tags you're interested in and only matching events show up (events with no tags at all are never hidden by it).

The gold ring on each card is the match: the booze score. A bright, glowing card is a confident pick (≥{{ (scoring_info.confident * 100) | int }}, capped at 3 per day); dimmed cards are maybes (≥{{ (scoring_info.maybe * 100) | int }}); anything below is hidden. Thresholds live in scoring/weights.py.
